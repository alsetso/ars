# Alternative Approaches - Completely Different Framing

## Current Problem
- "Programs" is generic
- Doesn't convey value or differentiation
- Feels like checkboxes rather than strategic offerings

---

## Alternative Approach #1: **"Who We Serve" / "Markets"**

### Framing: Market-Based Navigation
Instead of "programs," organize by who you serve.

**Structure:**
- **Municipal & Government**
- **Real Estate Professionals** 
- **Property Management Companies**
- **Homeowner Associations**
- **Commercial Property Owners**

**Why Smarter:**
- Customer-centric (they identify themselves first)
- Natural navigation (I'm a property manager ‚Üí I go here)
- No jargon, just clear categories
- SEO-friendly (people search "roofing for property managers")

**Page Title:** "Markets We Serve" or "Who We Work With"

---

## Alternative Approach #2: **"Solutions by Scale"**

### Framing: Organized by Project Complexity/Volume
Instead of market segments, organize by scale.

**Structure:**
- **Single Property Solutions** (Residential - standard)
- **Multi-Property Management** (Property Manager, HOA)
- **Portfolio Services** (Agent - multiple deals)
- **Enterprise Contracts** (Municipal, Commercial)

**Why Smarter:**
- Scales with customer growth
- Clear progression (start small, grow with us)
- Volume-based pricing makes sense
- Natural upsell path

**Page Title:** "Solutions by Scale" or "From Single Property to Enterprise"

## Key Insight

**The smartest approach removes the word "program" entirely** and frames it from the customer's perspective:
- What market am I in?
- What expertise do I need?
- Who do they work with?

Not: "What program do I join?"
